On The Trolley Problem

by Jesse Horne

According to Wikipedia, The Trolley Problem is a thought experiment in ethics. The situation is this. A runaway trolley is speeding down the tracks, headed towards a group of five people that are tied to the tracks. You are standing next to a lever that, when pulled, will change the trolleys tracks, saving the five people. The other set of tracks leads to one person, that is tied up. I've came to a conclusion about this problem and I would like to share my thoughts.

If I am not the cause of the trolley, speeding down the tracks, I can't be held responsible for any of the deaths that it will cause. Whoever tied the people to the tracks is to blame. If I pull the level, I can save five people, but I will be the cause of death for the other person. Some of you may think that this is a better option, and if you have a choice, saving four lives, and only ridding the world of one should be the correct way to solve the issue. This is wrong, in my opinion, because the moment you pull the level, you are engaging in the situation and becoming a part of the problem.

If you do nothing, the five people will die, but you are not to blame, because you didn't cause the trolley to be speeding down the tracks in the first place. If I were placed in this situation, I would do nothing. Yes, this would be a horrific experience, but inserting myself into the issue makes me a part of the death of the one, unfortunate person on the other set of tracks.

What would you do?
